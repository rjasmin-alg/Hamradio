# Activities

Amateur radio is far more than simple communication — it is a complete ecosystem of activities that connect technology, sport, nature, competition, and an international community. Whether you are interested in mountaineering, space, competitive operating, electronics or simply long conversations with people on the other side of the planet, there is something in this hobby for you.

This chapter covers the five main activities in which Croatian amateur radio operators are particularly active.

---

## Overview of Activities

### 🏆 [Contesting](contesting.md)

Organised amateur radio sport — the goal is to make as many contacts (QSOs) as possible within a set time frame. It sounds simple, but it requires operating speed, strategy, propagation knowledge, and a well-tuned station.

Contests run from a few hours to full 48-hour weekend events. Compare yourself against operators in the same category from around the world, then submit your log in the standard Cabrillo format at the end.

**Why try it:** Accelerates operating skills, forces you to understand propagation better, and delivers the adrenaline rush of a tight pile-up.

---

### 🌍 [DXing — Long-Distance Operating](dxing.md)

Hunting distant and rare stations — DXCC entities, of which there are approximately 340 on the entire planet. DXers monitor the DX cluster (a spotting network for rare active stations), enter pile-ups (crowds of operators calling the same rare DX station) and log confirmations via LoTW or paper QSL cards.

The DXCC award — confirming contacts with 100 different entities — is achievable by any serious DXer. The complete DXCC (all 340+) is the life's work of the most dedicated operators.

**Why try it:** Provides constant motivation for on-air activity — there is always a new entity to catch, a new expedition to chase, a new propagation opportunity.

---

### 🏔️ [Portable Operations — SOTA, POTA, IOTA](portable.md)

A combined experience of amateur radio and the outdoors:

- **SOTA** (Summits On The Air) — operating from mountain summits, scored by summit height
- **POTA** (Parks On The Air) — activations from national parks and protected areas
- **IOTA** (Islands On The Air) — operating from islands, especially popular in the Adriatic archipelago

Croatia is rich in SOTA locations (Velebit, Biokovo, Gorski Kotar, Medvednica), registered POTA parks (Plitvice, Krka, Paklenica…) and exceptionally attractive IOTA references (Palagruža is among the most sought-after EU references!).

**Why try it:** Combines hiking/excursions with radio, the sense of achievement from a successful activation, and QSOs with chasers worldwide who are waiting for you.

---

### 🛰️ [Satellite Communications](satellites.md)

Amateur radio operators have their own satellites in orbit — from FM repeaters accessible with a handheld radio, to advanced linear transponders for SSB and CW contacts. The ISS (International Space Station) has its own amateur radio station (NA1SS) operated manually by astronauts or automatically for APRS and SSTV.

**Why try it:** There is something fascinating about bouncing a signal off an object orbiting at 400 km altitude and hearing a response. The technical challenges of Doppler correction and antenna pointing make satellite work uniquely engaging.

---

### 🦊 [Amateur Radio Direction Finding — ARDF](ardf.md)

Also known as "fox hunting" — competitors use a receiver and directional antenna to locate hidden transmitters in woodland within a time limit. A combination of sport, navigation, and RF skill.

The Croatian national ARDF team has won multiple European and World medals — this is no small achievement. The discipline is especially popular among younger operators and youth (YOTA).

**Why try it:** For those who want physical activity combined with technology — ARDF is the perfect blend. And you will learn more about RF propagation through practical experience than from any textbook.

---

## Combined Activities

Many activities complement each other:

- **SOTA + IOTA**: Operating from a mountain summit on an island (e.g. Hvar or Krk have both SOTA summits and an IOTA reference) — a rare double activation
- **SOTA + Contesting**: Some operators activate SOTA summits during weekend contests — scoring points from both programmes simultaneously
- **DXing + POTA**: DX hunters don't skip portable activations from POTA locations, especially if the location is unusual or rare

## Activity Calendar (General)

| Season | Recommended Activities |
|---|---|
| **Spring (Mar–May)** | SOTA (without winter conditions), POTA, DXing on 10 m and 15 m (propagation improving) |
| **Summer (Jun–Aug)** | IOTA Contest (July), POTA, Sporadic-E VHF, ARDF competitions |
| **Autumn (Sep–Nov)** | CQ WW and major HF contests (!), DXing, SOTA winter bonus begins |
| **Winter (Dec–Feb)** | SOTA winter bonus, CW contests, DXing on 40 m and 80 m, satellites |
