# NATO Phonetic Alphabet

## Introduction
The primary reason for using the phonetic alphabet is reliable communication. In severe and difficult situations (noise, interference - QRM, and natural atmospheric static - QRN), words and letters may sound completely different or similar to one another (e.g., "B" and "V", or "M" and "N"). Substituting a whole internationally standardized word representing each individual character guarantees unambiguous understanding across the radio network.

## Numbers in Radio Traffic

Numbers are typically read normally in English, but with exceptions clarified here for audibility.

| Number | Standard Radio Pronunciation | Note |
|------|--------------------------|----------|
| **0** | Zero | Do not use "Oh". |
| **1** | One | — |
| **2** | Two | — |
| **3** | Three | — |
| **4** | Four | — |
| **5** | **Fife** | Pronounced as *Fife* to avoid confusion with "Nine" |
| **6** | Six | — |
| **7** | Seven | — |
| **8** | Eight | — |
| **9** | **Niner** | Pronounced as *Niner* to avoid confusion with "Five / Fife" |

!!! tip "Fife and Niner"
    Pay close attention to these two words and use them automatically. Because of "5" and "9" in standard RS reports (Readability and Signal), these are the two most important words among numbers.

## Spelling Examples

**Spelling Callsigns:**
- **9A1ZG** → "Niner Alpha One Zulu Golf"
- **W1AW** → "Whiskey One Alpha Whiskey"

**Spelling in a QSO:**
- "My name is JASMIN, Juliet Alpha Sierra Mike India November, over."

## Prosigns (CW and Voice)

Procedural signals (prosigns) shorten transmission time and clarify intentions.

| Prosign | CW Characters | Voice Equivalent | When to use |
|-----------|------------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **AR** | `.-.-.` | "End of message" | Used when the message is done, calling for a direct reply back. |
| **SK** | `...-.-` | "Silent Key" / "End of work" | Complete QSO. End of work, silence. |
| **BK** | `-...-.-`| "Break, over" | Fast pause between inputs for the other station to jump in. |
| **K** | `-.-` | "Over" | Calling literally everyone or returning the mic. |
| **KN** | `-.--.` | "Over to named only" | Exclusive call; only the named station has priority to answer. |
| **DN** | `-..-.`| "Do not answer" | Broadcasts or CQ messages testing propagation without wanting a reply. |

## Common Mistakes

- Saying `V as in Victor` instead of just `Victor`. This is a ← **HUGE MISTAKE!**. 
- The correct delivery is: "My callsign is *Niner Alpha*" and NEVER *"My callsign is nine, letter A as in Alpha"*. Do NOT drag the spelling.
- Do not use creative replacements (like "Zanzibar" instead of "Zulu" or "Xylophone" instead of "X-ray"). Zulu and X-Ray are mathematically proven to be distinct against noise.
