# Exam Preparation

## Introduction
Preparing for your amateur radio license exam requires more than just memorizing answers; it demands a solid understanding of the concepts so you can safely and effectively operate a radio station.

## Study Plan (2 to 3 Month Recommendation)

!!! tip "Daily Priority — Consistency Rule"
    Studying the material shouldn't be crammed. Doing 30-45 minutes of active learning **EVERY DAY** strengthens your knowledge significantly better than 4-hour weekend cram sessions.

## Content Summary

### Regulations and Licenses
Know your legal limits. Review [License Classes](../regulations/license_classes.en.md) and [National Regulations](../regulations/croatian_regs.en.md).
KEY TIP: The Golden Rule of Class A. To operate internationally with portability rights across CEPT nations, you must hold the Class A (HAREC) equivalent.

### Basic Electronics
Pure radio physics. Review [Electronics](../technical/electronics.en.md).
KEY TIP: Memorize three formulas: Ohm's Law ($U=R·I$), Power computation ($P=U·I$), and LC Resonant Frequency ($f=1/2\pi\sqrt{LC}$).

### Example Exam Questions (With explanations)

**Q1: Which license class allows the use of all amateur frequency bands?**  
**Answer:** Class A (CEPT Class).
> **Why?**: Lower classes restrict bands and bounds, whereas Class A provides global T/R 61-01 CEPT privileges.

**Q2: Resistors R1 = 100Ω and R2 = 150Ω are connected in series. Total resistance?**  
**Answer:** 250 Ω.
> **Why?**: In a series circuit, current goes through components consecutively. Their resistance adds up.

**Q3: Which component defines the resonant frequency in an LC circuit?**  
**Answer:** BOTH the inductor (L) and the capacitor (C). 
> **Why?**: The math equation for resonant frequency relies on the product of `L*C`. 

**Q4: Which Ionospheric layer is primary for global HF DX daytime propagation?**  
**Answer:** The **"F2"** layer at roughly 400 km.
> **Why?**: Lower D and E layers fade or absorb signals. F2 remains night and day for skip propagation.

**Q5: What does an SWR of 3:1 imply?**  
**Answer:** A mismatch returning up to 50% power back to the transmitter.
> **Why?**: Anything above 2:1 is bad and turns output energy into heat in your finals.

**Q6: What sideband is used below 10 MHz?**  
**Answer:** LSB (Lower Side Band).
> **Why?**: By international convention, below 10MHz LSB is used, above it USB is used.

**Q7: Why do power supply capacitors remain dangerous after pulling the plug?**  
**Answer:** They hold deadly high-voltage charges for up to 30 minutes.
> **Why?**: The discharging bleeder resistors take time, and touching them bridges the gap and unloads that voltage into your body.

## Exam Day Tips
- **Bring essentials:** ID, pens, allowable basic (non-programmable) calculators.
- **Tactic:** Read EVERY question to its absolute final comma. Eliminate obviously wrong options first.
- **Blocked/Panicking?** Leave the question blank, continue rolling through, and return to it later. Let the anxiety subside instead of rushing a guess.
