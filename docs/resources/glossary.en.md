## A
- **AF (Audio Frequency)**: [Audio frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audio_frequency). The frequency range audible to the human ear (approx. 20 Hz to 20 kHz).
- **AGC (Automatic Gain Control)**: [Automatic gain control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automatic_gain_control). A circuit that maintains a constant output level despite variations in input signal strength.
- **ALC (Automatic Level Control)**: A circuit in a transmitter that prevents overmodulation or overdriving the power amplifier.
- **AM (Amplitude Modulation)**: [Amplitude modulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amplitude_modulation). A modulation technique where information is encoded by varying the amplitude of the carrier wave.
- **APRS (Automatic Packet Reporting System)**: [APRS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automatic_Packet_Reporting_System). A system for real-time digital communications of information of immediate value (position, messages, telemetry).

## B
- **BCI (Broadcast Interference)**: Interference caused by broadcast stations (e.g., FM radio or TV).
- **BFO (Beat Frequency Oscillator)**: [Beat frequency oscillator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beat_frequency_oscillator). An oscillator in a receiver used to create an audible tone for CW and SSB reception.
- **BK (Break-in)**: [QSK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QSK_operation). The ability to hear the other station between your own Morse code characters (Full Break-in).

## C
- **CEPT**: [CEPT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Conference_of_Postal_and_Telecommunications_Administrations). European body that regulates the mutual recognition of amateur radio licenses.
- **CQ**: [CQ (call)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CQ_(call)). A general call to all stations ("Calling any station").
- **CW (Continuous Wave)**: [Continuous wave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuous_wave). A method of communication by keying a carrier on and off (Morse code).

## D
- **dB (Decibel)**: [Decibel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decibel). A logarithmic unit used to express the ratio of two values (e.g., power or voltage).
- **Dipole**: [Dipole antenna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dipole_antenna). The simplest type of antenna, consisting of two conductive elements, typically half a wavelength long in total.
- **Dummy Load**: [Dummy load](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dummy_load). A resistor (usually 50 ohms) connected to a transmitter instead of an antenna for testing purposes without radiating.
- **DX (Distance)**: [DXing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DXing). Abbreviation for distance or contact with a distant/rare station.

## E
- **EME (Earth-Moon-Earth)**: [EME communication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth%E2%80%93Moon%E2%80%93Earth_communication). A technique relying on the reflection of radio signals from the Moon.
- **ERP (Effective Radiated Power)**: [Effective radiated power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effective_radiated_power). The product of the power supplied to the antenna and its gain relative to a half-wave dipole.

## F
- **FM (Frequency Modulation)**: [Frequency modulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frequency_modulation). A modulation technique where information is encoded by varying the frequency of the carrier wave.
- **FT8**: [FT8](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WSJT_(amateur_radio_software)#FT8). A popular digital mode designed for weak-signal communication.

## G
- **GND (Ground)**: [Ground (electricity)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ground_(electricity)). Electrical connection to the earth for safety and reference potential.
- **GP (Ground Plane)**: [Monopole antenna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monopole_antenna). A vertical antenna (monopole) mounted over a ground plane (often radials) to simulate a perfect conductive surface.

## H
- **HF (High Frequency)**: [High frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_frequency). The radio frequency range from 3 to 30 MHz (Shortwave).
- **HT (Handheld Transceiver)**: [Walkie-talkie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walkie-talkie). A portable radio with battery and antenna in a single unit.

## I
- **IARU**: [International Amateur Radio Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Amateur_Radio_Union). An alliance of national amateur radio societies.
- **ITU**: [International Telecommunication Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Telecommunication_Union). The UN agency for information and communication technologies.

## L
- **LDS (Long Delayed Echo)**: [Long delayed echo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_delayed_echo). A phenomenon where a radio signal returns to the sender after a significant delay.
- **LSB (Lower Sideband)**: [Single-sideband modulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single-sideband_modulation). The lower sideband of an AM signal.

## M
- **MUF (Maximum Usable Frequency)**: [Maximum usable frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximum_usable_frequency). The highest frequency that can be used for communication between two points via ionospheric reflection.

## O
- **OM (Old Man)**: Slang for a male amateur radio operator, regardless of age. A friendly term.

## P
- **PA (Power Amplifier)**: [RF power amplifier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RF_power_amplifier). A device that increases the power of a radio signal before transmission.
- **PEP (Peak Envelope Power)**: [Peak envelope power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peak_envelope_power). The standard power measurement for SSB transmitters.
- **PTT (Push To Talk)**: [Push-to-talk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Push-to-talk). A switch that switches the device from receive to transmit mode.

## Q
*(See [Q-Codes](../operating/procedures.en.md#q-codes) for a detailed list)*
- **Q-code**: [Q code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Q_code). A standardized collection of three-letter codes.

## R
- **RIT (Receiver Incremental Tuning)**: A control that allows fine-tuning of the receive frequency without affecting the transmit frequency.
- **RST**: [R-S-T system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R-S-T_system). A system for reporting signal quality (Readability, Strength, Tone).
- **RTTY (Radioteletype)**: [Radioteletype](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radioteletype). A telecommunications system for transmitting text via radio.

## S
- **SWR (Standing Wave Ratio)**: [Standing wave ratio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standing_wave_ratio). A measure of impedance matching between the antenna and transmitter.
- **SSB (Single Sideband)**: [Single-sideband modulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single-sideband_modulation). An efficient voice transmission method using only one sideband.
- **Squelch**: [Squelch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Squelch). A circuit that suppresses the audio output of a receiver in the absence of a strong input signal.

## T
- **TVI (Television Interference)**: Interference caused by a radio transmitter to television reception.
- **TX**: Abbreviation for Transmitter or Transmitting.

## U
- **UHF (Ultra High Frequency)**: [Ultra high frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultra_high_frequency). The radio frequency range from 300 MHz to 3 GHz.
- **USB (Upper Sideband)**: [Single-sideband modulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single-sideband_modulation). The upper sideband of an AM signal.
- **UTC**: [Coordinated Universal Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time). The primary time standard by which the world regulates clocks and time.

## V
- **VFO (Variable Frequency Oscillator)**: [Variable-frequency oscillator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variable-frequency_oscillator). A component that allows continuous tuning of the frequency.
- **VHF (Very High Frequency)**: [Very high frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Very_high_frequency). The radio frequency range from 30 to 300 MHz.
- **VOX (Voice Operated Exchange)**: [Voice-operated switch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voice-operated_switch). A switch that operates when sound is detected.

## W
- **WARC**: [WARC bands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WARC_bands). Amateur radio bands allocated in 1979 (12m, 17m, 30m).
- **WPM (Words Per Minute)**: [Words per minute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Words_per_minute). A measure of telegraphy speed.

## X
- **XYL (Ex-Young Lady)**: Slang for the wife of a ham.

## Y
- **YL (Young Lady)**: Slang for a female ham operator.

## Z
- **Z (Zulu Time)**: [Zulu time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_zone#Zulu). Military/Aviation designator for UTC.
