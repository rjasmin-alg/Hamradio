# Radio Clubs

A radio club is the basic cell of the amateur radio community — a place where enthusiasts of all experience levels meet, share knowledge and equipment, organise field trips and competitions. You can join a radio club even without a licence — many people begin as guests or associate members while preparing for their exam.

---

## Why Join a Radio Club?

### 1. Access to Equipment

A serious amateur radio station — HF transceiver, antenna system, linear amplifier, rotator — can cost anywhere from €2,000 to €20,000 or more. A well-equipped club shack is available to all members. Many clubs have:
- HF stations with linear amplifiers
- Yagi or beam antennas on rotating towers
- VHF/UHF repeaters and digipeaters
- SDR setups for monitoring
- ARDF (direction finding) equipment for training

### 2. Mentoring and Education

Every club has veterans with decades of experience. Informal mentoring is a faster path than self-study:
- Exam preparation (many clubs run preparatory courses)
- Practical advice on your first station (what to buy, what to avoid)
- Guidance on antenna installation, SWR tuning, CAT control
- Introduction to specific activities (SOTA, FT8, contesting)

### 3. Social Aspect

Amateur radio can look like a solitary hobby, but in practice the QSO is just one form of interaction. Club meetings, field trips, team competitions — all of this builds friendships that last for decades.

### 4. Licensing Benefits

Collective membership in HRS through a club brings administrative benefits:
- Special club callsigns (e.g. 9A0X, 9A100HRS) available for special occasions
- Coordinated participation in contests (Multi-Op categories)
- Access to club repeaters and APRS infrastructure

!!! tip "Visit before you join"
    Almost all clubs welcome guests at club evenings and activities. Come along, meet people, see the equipment and atmosphere — and then decide. The amateur radio community is open and welcoming.

## What Happens at Radio Clubs?

Activities vary by club, but typically include:

- **Regular club meetings** — weekly or fortnightly, often with technical talks or guest presentations
- **Club activations** — S2S, SOTA, POTA, IOTA field trips using the club callsign
- **Contests** — Multi-Op teams for CQ WW, 9A DX Contest, and local events
- **Exam courses** — preparing candidates for the amateur radio exam
- **Flea markets (hamfest)** — annual or seasonal equipment hamfests
- **ARDF training** — direction finding under club organisation
- **Antenna workshops** — club construction of dipoles, Yagis, delta loops
- **Youth and YOTA** — club YOTA activities for operators under 26

## Club Callsigns and Special Events

Radio clubs can obtain a club callsign (e.g. `9A1RZG`) for contests and regular operations. In addition, for special occasions (anniversaries, World Amateur Radio Day, YOTA month) HRS assigns **special event callsigns** with thematic suffixes (e.g. `9A100HRS` for the 100th anniversary). DX hunters worldwide chase these callsigns — by participating you contribute to a global amateur radio event.

## Access for Unlicensed Beginners

!!! info "Can I join without a licence?"
    Yes — almost every club accepts people without an amateur licence as associate/guest members. As such, you may listen, practise on equipment under a licensed operator's supervision, and attend activities. You may transmit independently only once you hold a licence.
    A club is the ideal place to prepare for the exam.
