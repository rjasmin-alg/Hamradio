# Associations and Organisations

Amateur radio is regulated and organised at every level — from the local club to an international organisation that represents the hobby before the United Nations. Understanding this structure helps you know where to go for an exam, how to appeal a regulatory decision, or how to get involved in international programmes.

---

## HRS — Croatian Amateur Radio Association

**HRS** (Hrvatski radioamaterski savez — Croatian Amateur Radio Association) is the national umbrella organisation for all amateur radio operators in the Republic of Croatia, founded in 1946. HRS is a registered non-profit association, partially funded through the Ministry (as an association of special public interest) and through membership fees.

**Web:** [www.hamradio.hr](https://www.hamradio.hr)

### What Does HRS Do?

| Activity | Description |
|---|---|
| **Exams and licences** | Organises exam sessions for all amateur licence classes in cooperation with HAKOM |
| **QSL bureau** | Operates the national QSL bureau — receives and forwards cards to/from foreign bureaus |
| **Contests** | Organises or coordinates the 9A DX Contest, 9A Activity Contest, and represents Croatia in CQ WW and other international contests |
| **Frequency plan** | Coordinates with HAKOM on spectrum allocation and protection of amateur frequencies |
| **YOTA and youth** | Coordinates YOTA activities in Croatia, supports young operators |
| **ARDF** | Organises ARG competitions and selects and prepares the team for European and World championships |
| **Publications** | Publishes periodic bulletins and technical materials |
| **Representation** | Represents amateur radio operators before government bodies (HAKOM, Ministry) |

### How to Become a Member of HRS?

Two routes:

1. **Through a radio club** — join a local club that is a collective HRS member. This is the recommended route as you also gain all club benefits.
2. **Directly** — individual membership is possible without a club. Contact the HRS secretariat.

Annual membership fees for individual members are nominal (check the current amount on the website). A licence only requires passing the exam — HRS membership is not a prerequisite for a licence, but is recommended for access to all services.

## IARU — International Amateur Radio Union

**IARU** (International Amateur Radio Union) is the global umbrella organisation representing amateur radio operators before the **ITU** (International Telecommunication Union — the UN agency regulating radio spectrum use globally).

**Web:** [www.iaru.org](https://www.iaru.org)

IARU is divided into **3 regions** by geography:

| Region | Area | Secretariat |
|---|---|---|
| **Region 1** | Europe, Africa, Middle East, northern Asia | Geneva, Switzerland |
| **Region 2** | North and South America | — |
| **Region 3** | Asia and the Pacific | — |

**Croatia (HRS) is a full member of IARU Region 1.**

### What Does IARU Do?

- Represents amateur radio operators at ITU World Radiocommunication Conferences (WRC)
- Coordinates frequency plans (band plans) within regions — this is the source of the IARU Region 1 band plan we use
- Defines standards for amateur satellites (AMSAT)
- Coordinates the ARISS (Amateur Radio on the International Space Station) programme
- Defends amateur spectrum from commercial pressure

!!! note "Why IARU matters to you"
    Every time you see the IARU Region 1 band plan (which segment is for SSB, which for CW, which for digital modes) — that is the result of IARU's work. Without IARU, commercial interests would have long since encroached on the spectrum we use for FT8, SOTA, or satellites.

## ARRL — American Radio Relay League

**ARRL** is the US national amateur radio organisation, but has global relevance far beyond the USA:

- Administers the **DXCC programme** — the most important award in DXing, accepted globally
- Manages **LoTW** (Logbook of the World) — the digital QSO confirmation system
- Publishes **QST** magazine and the **ARRL Handbook**
- Organises **ARRL DX Contest** and many other contests

**Web:** [www.arrl.org](https://www.arrl.org)

As a non-US operator, ARRL is relevant to you for:
- Registering for LoTW (free)
- DXCC award applications
- Technical resources (Antenna Book, Handbook)

## DARC — Deutscher Amateur Radio Club

**DARC** is the German amateur radio organisation, one of the largest in Europe with over 40,000 members. Globally known for:

- **WAE DX Contest** (Worked All Europe)
- **CQ DL** magazine
- Technical publications and resources

**Web:** [www.darc.de](https://www.darc.de)

---

## RSGB — Radio Society of Great Britain

Relevant globally for:
- **IOTA Contest** (Islands On The Air) — oldest and one of the most popular portable contests
- **G-QRP Club** — QRP community, DIY projects
- Technical publications

**Web:** [www.rsgb.org](https://www.rsgb.org)

## Online Community Resources

Beyond formal organisations, these online resources form a coherent global community:

- **[QRZ.com](https://www.qrz.com)** — global operator database, callsign lookup, logs, forums
- **[DX Summit](https://www.dxsummit.fi)** — DX cluster, real-time spots
- **[Reddit r/amateurradio](https://reddit.com/r/amateurradio)** — active English-language forum
- **[HamSphere](https://www.hamsphere.com)** — amateur radio simulator (for practice without a licence)
