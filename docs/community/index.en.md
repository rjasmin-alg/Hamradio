# Community

Amateur radio is by its very nature a **social hobby**. Yes, you can sit alone in your shack and make contacts with the whole world — but the full dimension of the hobby only opens up through community: club fellowship, field trips, team competitions, mentoring, youth camps.

At every level — local, national and international — there are organisations that bring amateur radio operators together, protect the interests of the hobby, and organise activities.

## Why Does Community Matter?

### Knowledge is transmitted in person

Learning amateur radio from a book is possible, but slower. In a club there is always someone who has already "walked the path" — they know which antenna tuner to buy, how to adjust SWR, which software to install for FT8, which contest to recommend for beginners. Mentoring is informal, but worth more than any book.

### Shared equipment

A high-end station — a Yagi on a rotating tower, a linear amplifier, an SDR setup — costs tens of thousands of euros. A club can afford this; an individual rarely can. As a club member, you have access to equipment and infrastructure that would otherwise be out of reach.

### Exams and licensing

In Croatia, amateur radio exams are coordinated by **HRS** — the association organises exam sessions and provides materials. Many clubs run preparatory courses for candidates.

### Ham Spirit — the community's unwritten code

Almost every amateur radio operator will help you if you ask. Call a DX station and they know you are a Rookie — they will slow down the exchange. Your radio breaks on a SOTA summit — someone will lend you a spare. This culture of mutual support — "ham spirit" — predates the internet and lives in every QSO.

## Community Structure (Local to Global)

```
Individual amateur radio operator
        │
        ▼
   Radio club (local)
        │
        ▼
   HRS (Croatian Amateur Radio Association)
        │
        ▼
   IARU (International union)
   ├── Region 1 (Europe, Africa, Middle East)
   ├── Region 2 (Americas)
   └── Region 3 (Asia, Oceania)
```
