# CB Radio (Citizens Band)

Citizens Band (CB) radio is a publicly available radio service on frequencies around 27 MHz that requires **no exam or licence** to use — buy a radio, switch it on, and you can communicate. Despite this, there are technical regulations and unwritten rules of courtesy that users are expected to follow.

CB is popular among truck drivers, off-road enthusiasts, hikers, preppers, and anyone who needs reliable communication over short to medium distances without bureaucratic barriers.

## CB at a Glance

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Frequency range | 26.965 – 27.405 MHz |
| Number of channels | 40 |
| Modulations | AM, FM, SSB (LSB/USB) |
| Max. power (AM/FM) | 4 W |

| Typical range (base) | 15–50 km, DX hundreds of km |

## CB vs. Amateur Radio — Key Differences

CB and amateur radio share the same RF technology, but they are different services with different rules:

| Aspect | CB Radio | Amateur Radio |
|---|---|---|
| Exam / licence | ❌ Not required | ✅ Technical exam, licence |
| Max. power | 4 W (AM/FM), 12 W (SSB) | Up to 1,500 W (depending on licence class) |
| Frequency freedom | ❌ Only 40 fixed channels | ✅ Entire bands (HF, VHF, UHF…) |
| Modes | AM, FM, SSB | SSB, CW, FM, FT8, SSTV, and many more |
| Antenna | Regulated max. gain/height | Essentially unrestricted |
| DX capability | Occasional, propagation-dependent | Regular, worldwide |
| Infrastructure | None | Repeaters, satellites, ISS |
| Entry cost | Low (€50–200) | Medium (€200–1,000+) |

!!! tip "CB as a stepping stone"
    Many experienced amateur radio operators began on CB. They learned the basics of propagation, antennas, and radio etiquette, then passed the exam and moved on. If you are a CB user considering amateur radio — you are already well positioned!

## Propagation on 27 MHz

Although CB looks like a simple service, propagation at 27 MHz (11 m wavelength) can be surprisingly interesting:

- **Normal ranges:** A mobile station with 4 W achieves 5–15 km on flat terrain or in cities
- **Tropospheric propagation:** With favourable atmospheric conditions, ranges extend to 50–200 km
- **Sporadic-E (Es):** In spring and summer, the ionospheric E layer can reflect CB signals enabling contacts over **500–2,000 km** — this is CB DX

!!! info "The solar cycle and CB DX"
    Like the amateur 10 m band (28 MHz), CB is subject to solar influence. During solar maximum, F2 propagation can bring DX contacts of **5,000+ km** on 27 MHz. Experienced CB DXers monitor solar forecasts just like amateur radio operators!
