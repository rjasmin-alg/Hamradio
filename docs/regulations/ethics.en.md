# Amateur Radio Ethics and Operating Guidelines

The hobbyist and experimental radio spectrum is a precious natural resource. Amateur radio doesn't survive merely by the sheer technical force of laws forcing you to respect frequency; it survives by a "Gentleman's rule".

## The Amateur's Code (Paul M. Segal, W9EEA, 1928.)
The history and standard traces back to the inception of mass spectrum utilization framed through a legendary "Amateur's Code" displaying essential gold standards authored in 1928:

> **The Amateur is Considerate:** He never knowingly uses the air in such a way as to lessen the pleasure of others.
> **The Amateur is Loyal:** He offers his loyalty, encouragement and support to his fellow radio amateurs, his local club, and the League.
> **The Amateur is Progressive:** He keeps his station abreast of science. It is built well and efficiently.
> **The Amateur is Friendly:** Slow and patient sending when requested, friendly advice and counsel to the beginner, kindly assistance, cooperation, and consideration.
> **The Amateur is Balanced:** Radio is his hobby. He never allows it to interfere with any of the duties he owes to his home, his job, his school, or his community.
> **The Amateur is Patriotic:** His knowledge and his station are always ready for the service of his country and his community.

### 10 Proper Behavioral Rules Over Operating
Remember the etiquette sequence upon powering up:
1. Listen first! Never dump transmissions into a void without carefully scanning the background noise.
2. Verify "QRL?"! Transmit asking *"Is the frequency in use please?"*. You might save someone's exhausting hour-long QSO efforts.
3. Keep transmissions short. Avoid hour-long monologues. Let the partner input sentences in their loop.
4. Correct nicely. Don't aggressively yell at strangers regarding flawed phonetics. Calmly demonstrate proper NATO spelling instead.
5. Signal your ends. Explicitly utilize phrases like `Over`, `Clear`, or CW prosigns (`K` or `SK`).
6. Only use verified callsigns! Do NOT yell "Hey Tony pick it up!" Make sure to provide your callsign identifier regularly (every ten minutes rules depend heavily per country).
7. Drop power. Use the lowest Watt output sufficient to cover the needed space.
8. Wait out pile-ups. Don't bust in screaming your call if an expedition points fingers requesting specific tails.
9. Avoid ugly topics. Hardcore religion, deep volatile politics, or hostilities must be abandoned entirely out of gentlemanly respect for the hobby.
10. Welcome equality. Treat operators equally regardless of gender/position and embrace "Old Men" OM conventions.

## Sanctioned Behaviors (NEVER DO)

!!! warning "Broadcasting or Sales Pitches!"
    The station is NEITHER an FM advert radio, nor an internal selling tactic to order taxis. Broadcasting musical beats or using stations for business exchanges carries hundreds of euros in fines with outright irreversible suspensions!

## QRM/QRN Trolls (Intentional interference responses)

What if you hit a void while calling resulting in intentional interference, people playing digital buzz over you, or insulting the communication?
1. Stay utterly Silent. The moment you press key saying "Who is interfering?", the aggressor succeeded and will double down. Slide 5 kHz off the channel, ignoring the troll causes his expensive power amplifiers to basically burn completely useless.
2. File Reports. If you notice structural malicious usage, logging times alongside the IARU/HAKOM reports drives their inspection vehicles directly to finding the illegally radiating station!

## Priorities: Distress and MAYDAY Communications!

If within your chatter or scanning you hear specific urgent phrases utilized 3 times: **MAYDAY, MAYDAY MAYDAY**, **EMERGENCY EMERGENCY** or an SOS CW pattern `... --- ...`:

!!! danger "Stop and Evacuate the Spectrum Immediately!"
    Interrupting your chatter instantly is an absolute legally mandated action. Your sole duty becomes observing the distress communication. Attempt mapping their location completely. NEVER transmit anything hindering their rescue and only help redirecting emergency networks if explicitly requested to do so!

## Mentoring: Being an "Elmer" 
The classical "Elmer" term marks heavy gratitude targeting senior operatives taking terrified newcomers safely under their supportive wings ensuring the amateur craft survives. Teach them how to connect properly locally and respect laws without forcing expensive purchases upon them.
