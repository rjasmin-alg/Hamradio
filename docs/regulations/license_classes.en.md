# License Classes

Amateur radio licenses are split into a tier-based progression system. From beginner classes intended for local FM communications up to advanced expert tiers with full privileges and power limits on long wave HF communications.

## Fundamental Radio Amateur Classes 

### N Class (Novice – National Tier)
The N class is an extremely basic approach for younger members or complete trainees meant to operate almost exclusively upon entering club activities using handhelds.
- **Conditions:** Basic knowledge above local environments. High focus merely on simple operating safety.
- **Frequencies:** Purely short VHF/UHF bands (e.g. 144 MHz with repeaters). 
- **Modes:** Primarily Voice Analogs via FM using small unboosted output powers. Cannot utilize global reaching HF.

### B Class ("Novice" CEPT Tier)
In most central European nations, this class is the primary entry for adults. Synced with the Europe-wide "CEPT Novice", it relies on a serious test bringing basic mathematical electronics formulas.
- **Power & Bands:** Usually greenlights operations up to 100 Watts! Allowances include specially narrowed slices of shortwaves (80, 40, 20m) for international DX chasing. 

### A Class (CEPT HAREC – Advanced Tier)
Holding an A-Class signifies an advanced engineering engineering understanding. You hold **complete legal CEPT authorizations** based on "T/R 61-01".
- **Privilege & Output:** HAREC designation denotes your ability to completely construct and revamp components. You have unrestrained access to the whole ITU HF spectrum and can utilize powers scaling into the massive gigawatts (usually limited regionally from 1000 - 1500 Watts).

## Progressing as an Operator

After initiating the journey in Class B, you do not have to wait years to step up; subsequent tests allow you to skip straight to the HAREC tier if you possess the knowledge needed! Do note there are absolutely zero repercussions. Trying to shoot for the top Tier A test and missing the threshold, mostly gracefully slips you seamlessly down granting you the Novice Tier B anyway as a secondary pass reward! 

## Worldwide Equivalencies

What do you call an A-Class in America? 

=== "USA - FCC Classes"
    * **Technician:** N-class locally oriented on VHF.
    * **General:** Novice Tier CEPT counterpart. Massive global HF reach.
    * **Amateur Extra:** HAREC Expert Tier. Access to rare bandwidths.

=== "United Kingdom (Ofcom)"
    * **Foundation Licence:** Minimal practical entry around 10W limits.
    * **Intermediate:** Medium 50W output building phase.
    * **Full Licence:** HAREC designation with kilowatt-capable clearance.

=== "Germany"
    * **Klasse E:** CEPT Novice Tier with medium power.
    * **Klasse A:** HAREC Tier holding maximum station capabilities.
