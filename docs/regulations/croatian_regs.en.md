# Croatian Regulations (Hrvatski propisi)

Radio amateurism in Croatia is regulated by laws under the jurisdiction of state institutions to prevent abuse of the RF spectrum and ensure coordinated technical frameworks.

## Key Documents

### 1. Electronic Communications Act
This is the fundamental national law regulating the use of the electronic network and RF spectrum in Croatia for civil and military services. The main aspects for an amateur focus on the confiscation protocol of illegal gear and strict exposure guidelines (MPE/ICNIRP standards) related to constructed antennas.

### 2. Ordinance on Amateur Radio Communications
Consider this the crucial "bible of regulations". It dictates exactly what decibel power outputs and prefix bounds you adhere to as an active station.
- Establishes operators' classes and testing formats.
- Dictates boundaries of your permitted wave spectrum usage limits.

#### Call Sign Rules
In Croatia, all universal primary international identifiers start with the **`9A`** prefix.
Changing location temporarily holds obligations. You are required to amend suffixes during those operations:
* **`/P` (Portable):** For hiking, battery, or SOTA operations from temporary stations.
* **`/M` (Mobile):** While in motion on a road structure driving a car.
* **`/MM` (Maritime Mobile):** Operating communications from oceanic vessels/boats.
* **`/AM` (Aeronautical Mobile):** Transmitting RF if flying on a plane.

## Regulatory Authorities

### HAKOM (Croatian Regulatory Authority for Network Industries)
The primary inspecting arm. HAKOM sets the licensing testing, oversees the technical exams, and regulates limits. If you radiate too strongly creating high EMF interference issues in neighborhoods, their terrain vehicles hold the legal ability to confiscate your rig.

### HRS (Croatian Amateur Radio Association)
Your united hub - organizing global negotiations for the hobby. In terms of benefits natively, HRS acts worldwide saving members immense costs by handling all QSL cards out/in natively through the free "QSL bureau".

## Legislative History and Timeline

| Year | Milestone for Croatian Ham Operations |
|------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| 1992 | Implementation of the initial independent laws transitioning into the globally standardized `9A` prefix. |
| 2003 | Morse Code dropping globally removed limits for accessing the full HF band without prior CW knowledge. |
| 2012 | Implementations with CEPT T/R agreements bringing European synchronizations to low frequencies. |
| 2021 | Upgrading the exposure rules with heavier EMF requirements due to power density proximity laws. |

!!! warning "Agencies strictly forbid ignoring interference."
    Respecting the Electronic Communications Act is critical. Ignoring continuous harmful interference calls from your station is a definite way to permanently lose the license and have the equipment confiscated by HAKOM's legal jurisdiction!
