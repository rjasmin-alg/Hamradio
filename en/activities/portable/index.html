    Portable Operations — SOTA, POTA and IOTA

    Operating radio in the field is one of the most rewarding combined experiences in the hobby — a mountain summit, morning silence, and a QSO with Japan through a dipole made from wire and a fishing pole. Portable radio activism integrates nature, technology, and operating skills into a unique experience that no other activity quite matches.

    The three most organised portable activities are SOTA (mountain summits), POTA (parks and protected areas) and IOTA (islands). Each has its own scoring system, awards, and community of enthusiasts.

    SOTA — Summits On The Air

    SOTA is an international programme encouraging amateur radio operators to make contacts from registered mountain summits. The programme is coordinated by the Sota Mapping Project and organised through national associations (for Croatia: 9A SOTA).

    Activator and Chaser

    Two roles exist in the SOTA programme:

    • Activator — the operator who climbs the summit and makes contacts from there. Must be physically on the summit or within the "activation zone" (25 m vertically below the summit). Carries all equipment.
    • Chaser — an operator who contacts activators from their shack or any other location.

    For each successful activation, the activator must make a minimum of 4 QSOs. Duplicates with the same station do not count.

    Scoring and Awards

    Each SOTA summit carries a point value from 1 to 10, based on height and relative elevation gain:

    Award (Activator) Points
    Mountain Goat 1,000 points
    Super Goat 5,000 points
    Ultra Goat 10,000 points
    Award (Chaser) Contacts
    Shack Sloth 1,000 QSOs with SOTA summits

    Winter bonus: Activations between 1 November and 31 March earn double points for summits above 900 m. It pays to go out in winter!

    Summit-to-Summit (S2S): A contact between two simultaneously active SOTA summits earns a special bonus and is a memorable experience — coordinate with another activator and call each other from two peaks.

    Croatia in SOTA

    The 9A SOTA association covers all Croatian mountains. Popular targets:

    • Sljeme (9A/CA-001) — Medvednica, easily accessible, perfect for beginners
    • Obruč (9A/PR-005) — Gorski Kotar, excellent propagation westward
    • Velika Bielolasica (9A/PR-001) — highest peak in Gorski Kotar, 1,534 m
    • Sveti Jure (9A/DA-001) — Biokovo, 1,762 m, view over the Adriatic

    Full list of Croatian summits: SOTA database — 9A

    Alerts and Spots — Why They Matter

    An activator who announces the activation in advance and self-spots from the summit will attract far more chasers:

    • Alert — advance notice > 24 hours on SOTAwatch3. Chasers monitor alerts and will be waiting for you.
    • Spot — a public announcement saying "I am active now on X frequency". Self-spot via SMS, mobile app (SOTA Spotter, Reflector), or ask a chaser to spot you.

    No spot — no chasers

    An unannounced activation is possible, but you will be searching for QSOs in the dark. With a spot, chasers are already waiting and you can have 4 contacts within minutes. Always spot!

    POTA — Parks On The Air

    POTA is an international programme encouraging activations from national parks, nature parks, protected areas, and natural reserves. Less physically demanding than SOTA — you can often park your car 10 metres from the antenna.

    Rules

    • Operate from a registered park (database at pota.app)
    • Minimum 10 QSOs for one activation
    • The same park can be activated multiple times (each counts separately)
    • No time limit — even 30 minutes of operating from a park is valid

    Croatia and POTA

    The POTA database contains over 30 registered Croatian locations:

    • NP Plitvice Lakes (HR-0002)
    • NP Krka (HR-0004)
    • NP Paklenica (HR-0003)
    • PP Medvednica (HR-0010)
    • PP Vransko Lake (HR-0018)
    • ...and many more

    Full list of Croatian parks: POTA — Croatia

    POTA and repeated activations

    Unlike SOTA where a summit counts once a year per activator, the same POTA park can be activated every week. Many operators combine a walk in their local park with an hour of radio — an excellent combination.

    IOTA — Islands On The Air

    IOTA is the oldest of the three programmes, founded in 1964 in the UK (RSGB). The goal is to make contacts with islands — each island group has its own IOTA reference number (e.g. EU-016 for the Kvarner islands).

    Scoring

    • Chaser — collects IOTA references by working active island stations. The award starts at 100 confirmed IOTA references.
    • Activator — operates from an island and provides confirmations to chasers. Activation requires a minimum of 100 QSOs.

    Croatia and IOTA

    The Adriatic is a wealth of IOTA references:

    IOTA Reference Island group Notes
    EU-016 Kvarner islands (Krk, Cres, Lošinj, Rab…) Regularly activated
    EU-110 North Dalmatian islands (Zadar archipelago) Active
    EU-136 South Dalmatian islands (Šibenik, Trogir, Split area) Active
    EU-016 Korčula, Hvar, Brač Active
    EU-090 Palagruža Extremely rare! Every activation draws a pile-up

    Palagruža (EU-090) is a small rocky island in the middle of the Adriatic, 68 km from Korčula. Inaccessible, rare, sought-after — every activation becomes a European-scale DX event.

    Power

    Battery Capacity Weight Runtime (5 W TX/RX) Notes
    LiFePO4 3 Ah 3 Ah / 12 V ~250 g ~3 hours Short SOTA activations
    LiFePO4 5 Ah 5 Ah / 12 V ~380 g ~5 hours Standard choice
    LiFePO4 10 Ah 10 Ah / 12 V ~840 g ~10 hours Longer POTA sessions
    Solar panel 10 W — ~700 g Unlimited (with sun) Excellent for summer POTA

    LiFePO4 is the portable standard

    Lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) batteries are superior to everything else for portable use: lightweight, safe (no swelling or fire like Li-Ion), long cycle life (2,000+), and stable voltage. Buy once, use for years.

    Activation Checklist

    The day before: - [ ] Check the weather forecast (especially for SOTA — lightning on a summit is deadly!) - [ ] Post a SOTA alert at sotawatch.sota.org.uk - [ ] Charge all batteries fully - [ ] Test the antenna and cables at home

    Day of activation: - [ ] Radio, battery, antenna, cables - [ ] Phone with mobile data (for spotting) - [ ] Log notebook and pen (or app) - [ ] Food, water, appropriate clothing - [ ] For SOTA: terrain map, compass (or GPS), first aid kit

    On location: - [ ] Set up antenna and check SWR - [ ] Post your spot immediately after setup - [ ] Log each QSO (date, UTC, callsign, frequency, mode, RST) - [ ] For SOTA: minimum 4 QSOs

    Lightning on SOTA summits

    At the first signs of thunder or dark clouds — immediately remove the antenna and descend. A metal antenna on an exposed mountain summit is a catastophic combination. No QSO is worth more than safety. Mountain weather can arrive in minutes without warning.
