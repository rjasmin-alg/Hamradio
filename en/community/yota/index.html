    YOTA — Youngsters On The Air

    YOTA (Youngsters On The Air) is an international initiative of IARU Region 1 with a clear objective: to attract young people to amateur radio and keep them active in the hobby. The programme is open to operators up to 26 years of age — regardless of whether they already hold a licence or are simply interested.

    Croatia is one of the most active YOTA countries in Europe, with regular participation in international camps and excellent competition results.

    Why Does YOTA Exist?

    Amateur radio is a hobby with a tradition of over 100 years — but like any hobby, it needs to attract new generations. IARU recognised that without actively bringing in young people, the average age of operators rises continually. YOTA is the response to that challenge.

    The statistic that drives action

    In some European countries the average age of a licensed amateur radio operator exceeds 60. The YOTA programme, launched in 2011, is now active in 50+ countries and involves thousands of new young operators every year.

    YOTA Activities

    YOTA Summer Camp

    The most important annual YOTA event — a week-long camp held in a different European country each year:

    • 50–100 young operators from 30+ countries participate
    • Technical amateurs from the host organisation lead workshops and lectures
    • Active club stations, contests, DXCC hunting, antenna building workshops
    • A strong social element — friendships that last for years
    • Accommodation and programme are subsidised (HRS pays or co-funds costs for Croatian representatives)

    Croatia has previously hosted the YOTA Summer Camp, and young Croatian operators regularly participate in camps abroad.

    YOTA Month (December)

    Every year in December, hundreds of operators across Region 1 activate special club callsigns with the suffix YOTA (e.g. 9A00YOTA, DL0YOTA, G0YOTA):

    • DX hunters worldwide chase these callsigns — massive interest
    • Young operators staff the stations and log contacts
    • Special certificates for hunters who collect YOTA callsigns

    From a hunter's perspective: collect QSOs with YOTA callsigns and submit your log through the YOTA web system.

    YOTA Contest

    An amateur radio contest tailored for young people, featuring: - A special category for Rookie and YOTA operators - A simpler exchange (appropriate for beginners) - Promotion of CW mode (Morse telegraphy) among younger generations

    Other YOTA Activities

    • JOTA (Jamboree on the Air) — annual event where Scouts worldwide communicate via amateur radio (always the third weekend of October)
    • Amateur radio in schools — YOTA projects bringing amateur radio operators into schools for demonstrations
    • Mentoring — experienced YOTA alumni mentor new members

    YOTA in Croatia

    The Croatian YOTA community operates through HRS and local radio clubs. Particularly active in:

    • Contests — young Croatian operators regularly place highly in Rookie and YOTA categories
    • SOTA activations — YOTA + SOTA combination is popular; hiking and radio in one
    • ARDF competitions — Croatia's direction finding team has a strong youth component
    • YOTA camp participation — Croatian candidates are selected through HRS and club recommendations

    How to get involved with YOTA in Croatia: 1. Contact HRS secretariat or a local YOTA coordinator (hamradio.hr) 2. Join a local radio club with an active youth section 3. Follow YOTA activities at www.ham-yota.com 4. Apply for the summer camp through HRS (places are limited — apply early!)

    No licence? No problem!

    YOTA is open to people without an amateur licence. At camps and club activities you can participate, listen, and practise under a licensed operator's supervision. YOTA is an excellent motivator for taking the exam.

    How to Get Started as a Young Amateur Radio Operator

    If you are under 26 and amateur radio interests you — here is the shortest path:

    1. Find a local radio club — HRS listing — visit as a guest
    2. Take the exam — the club will help you prepare; the exam is achievable with 2–3 months of study
    3. Get your callsign — and make your first contact!
    4. Sign up for YOTA activities — camps, YOTA month, contests
    5. Try portable radio — SOTA or POTA are excellent for young people who enjoy the outdoors
    6. Learn CW — Morse code is a valuable skill and unlocks a wealth of contest categories
